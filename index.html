Ekiti State

Ekiti State, in southwestern Nigeria, is known for its rolling hills, rich cultural heritage, and dedication to education. Created in 1996, with Ado-Ekiti as its capital, the state is primarily agrarian, producing crops like cocoa and yams. Known as "The Land of Honor and Integrity," Ekiti is home to notable attractions like the Ikogosi Warm Springs and the Erin Ayonigba Sacred Fish River, making it a significant cultural and tourist destination in Nigeria.
